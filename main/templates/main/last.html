Kokpar

Kokpar is an ancient equestrian sport in which horse-mounted players attempt to advance a headless goat carcass towards the opposing team's goal. The national game is known as kokpar in Kazakhstan, buzkashi in Afghanistan, Tajikistan and the U.S., kokboru and ulaq tartysh in Kyrgyzstan and kupkari in Uzbekistan

Asyk

Asyk atu is a popular Kazakh national game which means to beat out with one's own asyk (a bone of a knee sheep joint) or several asyks at a certain distance, which then become the property of a player. Since 2017 it has been part of UNESCO's Representative List of the Intangible Cultural Heritage of the Humanity.

Tusau kesu

Image result for kazakh tradition tusau kesu One of the most important rituals in Kazakh families is Tusau Kesu. This is cutting of the fetters on child's feet so that their future road in life is easy and bright. From the earliest times until today, the Kazakh people have devoted a lot of time to raising children.

First Olympic medalIn 1960, Kazakh sprinter Husuman Kuoshanov competed for the Soviet Union at the Rome Olympics and won a silver medal in the 4x100m relay. It was also the first Olympic medal won by a Kazakh athlete.

First Olympic Gold Medal (pre-independence)At the 1980 Moscow Olympics, two Kazakh Greco-Roman wrestlers, Jakslak Ushkenpulvov and Shymur Selikov, won two gold MEDALS for the Soviet Union. He also became the first Kazakh athlete to win a gold medal.

Kazakhstan winter Olympics legendIn 1994, Kazakhstan officially participated in the seventeenth Winter Olympic Games as an independent country. And Kazakh Winter Olympics legend Vladimir Smirnov, born in Kukoshetou, Akamora, won kazakhstan's first gold medal as an independent. His 1 gold, 2 silver and 1 bronze MEDALS put Kazakhstan 12th on the medal list

Boxing powerKazakhstan is known as a boxing powerhouse, both professional and amateur, and even among the powerful former Soviet republics, kazakhs have few rivals. Until Rio 2016, the glorious tradition of Kazakh boxers averaging at least one gold medal in each Olympic Games continued. The domestic cult of the project's heroes has never stopped. Under the personal care of Kazakhstan's first President Nazarbayev, boxing has grown from a small sport to a large sport and has developed into one of the business cards of Kazakhstan to spread its national image to the outside world.

Kazakhstan's sporting prowessBesides China, Japan and South Korea, Kazakhstan sports has a great gap in reputation and strength among the "second echelon" of Asian competitive sports. Kazakhstan and Iran have long alternated fourth place in the Asian Games gold medal table. From the 1994 Hiroshima Asian Games to the Jakarta Asian Games, Kazakhstan has won 155 gold MEDALS in seven games, which is the average number of gold MEDALS won per games after China, Japan and South Korea.
