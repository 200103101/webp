Kokpar

Kokpar is an ancient equestrian sport in which horse-mounted players attempt to advance a headless goat carcass towards the opposing team's goal. The national game is known as kokpar in Kazakhstan, buzkashi in Afghanistan, Tajikistan and the U.S., kokboru and ulaq tartysh in Kyrgyzstan and kupkari in Uzbekistan

Asyk

Asyk atu is a popular Kazakh national game which means to beat out with one's own asyk (a bone of a knee sheep joint) or several asyks at a certain distance, which then become the property of a player. Since 2017 it has been part of UNESCO's Representative List of the Intangible Cultural Heritage of the Humanity.

Tusau kesu

Image result for kazakh tradition tusau kesu One of the most important rituals in Kazakh families is Tusau Kesu. This is cutting of the fetters on child's feet so that their future road in life is easy and bright. From the earliest times until today, the Kazakh people have devoted a lot of time to raising children.

Country Name: The Republic of Kazakhstan.

Population: 18.877 million (as of January 2021). There are about 140 ethnic groups, of which the Kazakh account for about 70% and the Russian about 18%.

Capital: Nur-Sultan (нур -султан, formerly Astana, changed its name in March 2019) Has average temperatures of -19℃ to -4℃ in January and 19℃ to 26℃ in Jul

Head of State: Kassym-Jomart Tokayev (Касым-Жомарт Токаев).

Official language: Kazakh is the national language. Kazakh and Russian are the official languages.

Currency: Tengo.

Brief: Located in central Asia. It borders Russia in the north, Uzbekistan, Turkmenistan and Kyrgyzstan in the south, the Caspian Sea in the west and China in the east. The majority of residents follow the Muslim (Sunni) religion. In addition, there are eastern Orthodox, Catholic and Buddhist. The Republic of Kazakhstan proclaimed its independence on 16 December 1991.

Politica: Political stability. On March 19, 2019, nazarbayev, the first president, announced his resignation in a televised address. On March 20, Kazakhstan's parliament held a joint session, and the former speaker of the upper house of parliament Tokayev was sworn in as the new president. On June 9th Mr Tokayev won an unscheduled presidential election.

Party: There are six registered political parties in the Ministry of Justice: The Light of the Fatherland Party, the People's Party, the Bright Way Democratic Party, the National Social Democratic Party, the Agricultural and Rural Party, and the Integrity Party.
